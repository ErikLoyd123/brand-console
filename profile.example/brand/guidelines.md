# Harbor Roasters — brand guidelines (example)

This file shows the **brand documents** convention: drop any `.md` or `.html` file
directly into `brand/` — a company brand book, a tone-of-voice guide, a messaging
doc, an exported style page — and skills that produce brand-facing work read it
before producing anything. Use as many files as you have; they're read in full.
Entirely optional, and most useful for a brand (company) profile where formal
guidelines already exist somewhere else — paste or export them here.

Everything below is fictional example content.

## Who we are

Harbor Roasters is a small-batch coffee roastery. We talk like a roaster at the
bar, not a beverage corporation: specific, warm, a little nerdy about process,
never breathless.

## Tone

- Plain words over industry jargon; explain a term the first time it appears.
- Confident but never superlative — "our best crop this year", not "the world's
  finest beans".
- Numbers beat adjectives: roast dates, origin altitudes, cup scores.
- Humor is dry and rare; never punny.

## Visual voice

- Warm, analog, tactile: hands, machines, burlap, steam.
- Brown/crema palette (see `brand.yaml`); whitespace is generous.
- Photography looks like our actual roastery on a working day — never sterile
  stock offices, never latte-art clichés.

## Words we avoid

"Artisanal", "curated", "elevated", "journey", "experience" (as a noun for
drinking coffee).
